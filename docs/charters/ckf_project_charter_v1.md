# Career Knowledge Framework (CKF) Project Charter

**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Draft – Authoritative Foundation
**Project Type:** Documentation-First Knowledge Framework

---

# Problem Statement

Professional experience is commonly represented through disconnected documents such as resumes, cover letters, LinkedIn profiles, portfolios, job applications, interview notes, and personal records.

Because these artifacts are typically maintained independently, they inevitably drift over time. Information becomes duplicated, inconsistent, difficult to verify, and increasingly expensive to maintain. Changes made in one location are often not reflected elsewhere, resulting in conflicting representations of the same professional history.

The Career Knowledge Framework (CKF) addresses this problem by establishing a single authoritative representation of professional experience from which all downstream career artifacts can be derived.

---

# Purpose

The Career Knowledge Framework is an evidence-based methodology for transforming human experience into structured, governed career knowledge.

Rather than treating resumes and professional profiles as primary artifacts, CKF treats them as derivative representations generated from an authoritative foundation.

The framework is designed to:

* Preserve the provenance of professional experience.
* Improve consistency across career artifacts.
* Reduce duplication of effort.
* Support long-term maintenance of professional knowledge.
* Enable transparent, evidence-based career representation.

---

# Scope

CKF defines the methodology, governance, and artifact architecture required to organize professional experience into reusable knowledge.

The framework includes:

* Professional employment history.
* Formal education.
* Professional development.
* Independent research and development.
* Supplemental experience contributing to demonstrated capability.
* Capability extraction methodology.
* Capability assessment methodology.
* Presentation methodology for downstream career artifacts.

CKF intentionally focuses on representation and governance rather than career advice or recruiting strategy.

---

# Non-Goals

The Career Knowledge Framework does **not** attempt to:

* Replace personal judgment in career decisions.
* Predict hiring outcomes.
* Recommend career paths.
* Invent accomplishments, responsibilities, or capabilities not supported by available evidence.
* Replace resumes or portfolios as externally consumed documents.
* Define software implementation, automation, schemas, or AI tooling during the initial framework phase.

---

# Core Artifact Chain

CKF organizes professional knowledge through a governed chain of artifacts.

```
Experience
        │
        ▼
Master Experience Ledger (MEL)
        │
        ▼
Capability Pattern & Insight Ledger (CPIL)
        │
        ▼
Career Capability Assessment (CCA)
        │
        ▼
Resume Generation Framework (RGF)
        │
        ▼
Targeted Career Artifacts
```

Each artifact consumes governed information from upstream artifacts while producing increasingly specialized downstream representations.

No downstream artifact supersedes or replaces its upstream authority.

---

# Guiding Principles

The framework is governed by several foundational principles:

* Maintain a single authoritative source for professional experience.
* Prefer evidence over recollection whenever evidence is available.
* Preserve traceability between artifacts.
* Separate facts, interpretation, and presentation.
* Record revisions through version-controlled artifacts.
* Revise information when stronger evidence becomes available.
* Avoid unsupported inference or capability inflation.
* Design artifacts for long-term maintainability rather than one-time document generation.

---

# Intended Users

CKF is intended for:

* Individual professionals maintaining long-term career records.
* Career coaches and mentors.
* Recruiters and hiring professionals interested in structured capability representation.
* Researchers studying professional capability development.
* Developers building career-management tooling.
* AI systems requiring governed professional knowledge as an authoritative input.

---

# Relationship to the blAIne Ecosystem

The Career Knowledge Framework applies the broader principles of governance, evidence, traceability, and structured knowledge representation developed throughout the blAIne ecosystem to the domain of professional experience.

Although developed within that ecosystem, CKF is designed as a standalone framework that can be adopted independently.

---

# Current Status

CKF is currently in its foundational phase.

The immediate objective is to establish stable governance, methodology, artifact definitions, and documentation before introducing automation or software implementation.

Future phases may include:

* Formal artifact specifications.
* Validation schemas.
* Reference implementations.
* Automation tooling.
* AI-assisted artifact generation.
* Community contributions and adoption.

The framework will remain documentation-first until the governing methodology has reached sufficient maturity.
