# Career Capability Assessment (CCA) Specification

**Artifact:** Career Capability Assessment (CCA)  
**Framework:** Career Knowledge Framework (CKF)  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Draft – Authoritative Artifact Specification

---

## Purpose

The Career Capability Assessment (CCA) is the authoritative interpretation and assessment layer for Capability Knowledge within the Career Knowledge Framework.

The CCA evaluates Capability Knowledge recorded in the Capability Pattern & Insight Ledger (CPIL), with relevant Master Experience Ledger (MEL) context where appropriate, to explain meaning, strengths, limits, transferability, specialization, progression, and career alignment.

The CCA is intended to answer one fundamental question:

> **What does this individual's authoritative Capability Knowledge indicate about their professional direction, fit, strengths, and development patterns?**

---

## Authority

The CCA is authoritative for interpretation and assessment of Capability Knowledge.

The CCA is not authoritative for experience records. Experience authority remains exclusively with the MEL.

The CCA is not authoritative for Capability Knowledge itself. Capability Knowledge authority remains with the CPIL.

The CCA consumes CPIL records and relevant MEL context without modifying either artifact.

Interpretations produced by the CCA must remain traceable to authoritative upstream artifacts.

Authority flows through the Career Knowledge Framework in the following order:

```text
MEL -> CPIL -> CCA -> RGF
```

Changes to experience information originate in the MEL. Changes to Capability Knowledge originate in the CPIL. Changes to assessment interpretation originate in the CCA only when they remain grounded in authoritative upstream artifacts.

---

## Scope

The CCA may assess:

- Demonstrated capability patterns
- Capability clusters
- Strengths and distinguishing characteristics
- Capability maturity and depth
- Capability breadth and range
- Transferability across roles, domains, and contexts
- Specialization and generalization
- Progression over time
- Recency and continuity
- Career alignment
- Role alignment
- Domain alignment
- Constraints, limitations, and ambiguity
- Gaps relative to a stated career direction or role context
- Evidence quality and interpretive confidence

The CCA may synthesize meaning across multiple CPIL records and relevant MEL context, provided each interpretation remains traceable to the records that support it.

---

## Non-Goals

The CCA does not:

- Record primary experience history.
- Replace or override the MEL.
- Create or modify Capability Knowledge.
- Replace or override the CPIL.
- Invent capabilities or experience.
- Generate resumes.
- Select final resume language.
- Present itself as a public-facing profile by default.
- Predict hiring outcomes.
- Make unsupported claims about seniority, mastery, or fit.
- Treat assessment conclusions as immutable facts.

Those responsibilities belong to the MEL, CPIL, RGF, or other downstream artifacts as appropriate.

---

## Core Principles

The CCA is governed by the following principles:

### Downstream of CPIL

The CCA depends on the CPIL for Capability Knowledge authority.

No CCA interpretation may establish a capability that is absent from, or unsupported by, the CPIL.

### Contextualized by MEL

The CCA may use relevant MEL context to understand the experience basis, timing, duration, environment, or outcomes behind CPIL capabilities.

MEL context informs interpretation but remains governed by the MEL.

### Interpretation Authoritative

The CCA is the canonical CKF source for governed interpretation and assessment of Capability Knowledge.

Downstream artifacts should use the CCA when they need assessed meaning, positioning, alignment, or interpretive guidance rather than raw Capability Knowledge alone.

### Traceable

Each assessment should identify the CPIL records and, where relevant, MEL entries that support it.

Traceability allows downstream artifacts to distinguish assessment interpretation from source experience and Capability Knowledge.

### Conservative

The CCA should avoid overstating capability depth, breadth, recency, seniority, specialization, or transferability.

Interpretation should remain proportional to the authoritative records that support it.

### Transparent

The CCA should make uncertainty, assumptions, limits, and confidence visible.

When the record supports more than one reasonable interpretation, the CCA should preserve that ambiguity rather than collapse it into unsupported certainty.

### Revision Controlled

The CCA evolves through documented revisions.

Assessment records should remain explainable as upstream MEL and CPIL records are added, clarified, split, merged, retired, or revised.

### Long-Term

The CCA is intended to support long-term career understanding rather than only a single job search, resume, or application.

## Assessment Dimensions

The CCA may assess Capability Knowledge across dimensions such as:

- Strength
- Depth
- Breadth
- Recency
- Duration
- Frequency
- Progression
- Complexity
- Autonomy
- Leadership
- Collaboration
- Domain specificity
- Cross-domain applicability
- Transferability
- Evidence support
- Confidence
- Limitations
- Career alignment

Assessment dimensions are interpretive lenses.

They do not create experience authority, create Capability Knowledge authority, or permit conclusions unsupported by upstream artifacts.

---

## Assessment Structure

Each CCA entry should describe an interpretation, assessment, or finding.

An assessment may contain:

- Assessment title
- Assessment type or dimension
- Assessment statement
- Supporting CPIL record references
- Relevant MEL context references, when appropriate
- Pattern or rationale
- Career or role context, when applicable
- Strengths identified
- Transferability notes
- Limitations or constraints
- Confidence notes
- Evidence considerations
- Recommended downstream use
- Revision history

Individual implementations may extend these fields provided they preserve upstream traceability and the CCA's authority over interpretation only.

---

## Pattern Identification

Pattern identification is the process of recognizing meaningful assessment relationships across CPIL Capability Knowledge and relevant MEL context.

The CCA may identify patterns such as:

- Repeated capabilities across multiple experiences
- Increasing responsibility or complexity over time
- Recurring domains, tools, methods, or environments
- Consistent strengths across different contexts
- Combinations of capabilities that suggest a professional orientation
- Concentrated specialization in a particular area
- Broad generalist capability across multiple areas
- Shifts in career direction or capability emphasis
- Gaps, discontinuities, or unclear areas in the record

Patterns must be derived from authoritative upstream artifacts.

The CCA may describe what a pattern suggests, but it must not convert a suggestion into a factual claim beyond what the CPIL and MEL support.

---

## Transferability

Transferability is the assessment of whether capabilities demonstrated in one context may reasonably apply in another context.

The CCA may assess transferability across:

- Roles
- Industries
- Domains
- Organizations
- Team structures
- Project types
- Tools and platforms
- Methods and workflows
- Levels of responsibility
- Career directions

Transferability assessments should identify the basis for transfer, the target context, the degree of confidence, and any limits.

The CCA must not treat transferability as proof of direct experience in a new context unless that experience is recorded in the MEL.

---

## Confidence and Limits

The CCA may describe confidence and limits associated with an assessment.

Confidence should reflect:

- Strength of CPIL support
- Specificity of related Capability Knowledge
- Quality and relevance of MEL context
- Recency and duration of supporting experience
- Repetition across multiple records
- Availability of supporting evidence
- Clarity of outcomes or responsibilities
- Degree of inference required

Limits should be recorded when an assessment depends on narrow evidence, incomplete context, older experience, indirect support, limited scale, or assumptions about transferability.

Confidence must not be treated as a substitute for traceability.

Limitations do not invalidate an assessment by themselves, but they should shape how downstream artifacts use it.

---

## Inputs

The CCA accepts information from:

- Capability Pattern & Insight Ledger Capability Knowledge
- CPIL revision history
- MEL entries referenced by CPIL records
- MEL context relevant to timing, scope, outcomes, and experience setting
- Supporting evidence referenced by upstream artifacts
- Career, role, or domain context supplied for assessment purposes
- Clarifications from the individual when tied back to authoritative upstream records

The CCA must not accept untraceable experience or capability claims as authoritative assessment inputs.

---

## Outputs

The CCA provides governed interpretation to:

- Resume Generation Framework (RGF)
- Position-specific resumes
- LinkedIn profiles
- Professional portfolios
- Career analyses
- Career planning artifacts
- Interview preparation artifacts
- Future CKF artifacts

CCA outputs may include:

- Strength assessments
- Capability pattern summaries
- Transferability assessments
- Specialization assessments
- Career alignment assessments
- Role alignment assessments
- Gap and limitation notes
- Confidence and uncertainty notes
- Downstream positioning guidance

Outputs from the CCA should preserve traceability to CPIL records and relevant MEL context wherever practical.

---

## Relationship to Other Artifacts

The CCA sits downstream of the CPIL and upstream of resume generation and other presentation artifacts.

```text
Individual
      |
      v
Master Experience Ledger (MEL)
      |
      v
Capability Pattern & Insight Ledger (CPIL)
      |
      v
Career Capability Assessment (CCA)
      |
      v
Resume Generation Framework (RGF)
      |
      v
Career Artifacts
```

The MEL records experience.

The CPIL records Capability Knowledge derived from experience.

The CCA interprets and assesses Capability Knowledge in career, role, domain, and developmental contexts.

The RGF transforms authoritative experience, capability pattern, insight, and governed assessment information into presentation-specific career artifacts.

---

## Prohibited Behavior

The CCA must not:

- Invent experience.
- Invent capabilities.
- Modify MEL entries.
- Modify CPIL records.
- Treat assessment interpretation as experience authority.
- Treat assessment interpretation as Capability Knowledge authority.
- Represent inferred transferability as direct experience.
- Overstate seniority, mastery, scope, or certainty.
- Convert weak or narrow upstream support into broad assessment claims.
- Present speculative, aspirational, or desired directions as established capabilities.
- Treat absence of evidence as proof that a capability or pattern does not exist when upstream records reasonably support it.
- Generate final resume content as if it were the RGF.
- Override the CKF authority flow.

---

## Revision Behavior

The CCA should be revised when:

- CPIL records are added, removed, clarified, split, merged, or corrected.
- MEL entries relevant to an assessment are revised.
- Capability interpretations become more precise.
- Career, role, or domain contexts change.
- Supporting evidence becomes available.
- Confidence or limitation notes need refinement.
- Downstream artifacts reveal ambiguity that should be resolved at the assessment level.

Revision should preserve the relationship between each assessment and the CPIL records and MEL context that support it.

When an upstream change invalidates an assessment, the CCA should revise, retire, or qualify the affected assessment rather than preserve it as an unsupported interpretation.

---

## Long-Term Role

The CCA is intended to become the durable interpretation layer of the Career Knowledge Framework.

Over time, it should make an individual's professional direction, strengths, development patterns, and capability transferability easier to understand without repeatedly reassessing raw experience and Capability Knowledge.

The CCA supports long-term career knowledge management by separating source records from governed interpretation while preserving traceability to authoritative upstream artifacts.

---

## Summary

The Career Capability Assessment is the authoritative CKF artifact for interpretation and assessment of Capability Knowledge.

It remains downstream of the Capability Pattern & Insight Ledger and may use relevant Master Experience Ledger context without modifying either artifact.

The CCA may identify patterns, transferability, strengths, limitations, specialization, progression, and career alignment.

It must not invent capabilities or experience, and every interpretation must remain traceable to authoritative upstream artifacts.

The CCA provides governed assessment meaning used by resume generation, career analysis, planning, positioning, and future career artifacts.
